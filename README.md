💛 Remember Today — Caregiver Guide
A gentle memory game for your loved one, personalised with your own photos.
What is Remember Today?
Remember Today is a single file — RememberToday.html — that runs in any web browser on a phone, tablet, or computer. No internet needed after the first open. No app to install. No login. Just one file, saved on your device.
It contains six games designed for people living with dementia:
Game
What it does
🏠 Journey Home
Tap numbered dots to trace a heart-shaped walk home
🌳 Walk in the Park
A longer version of the same walk
🌟 Memory Match
Flip cards and find matching pairs
👨‍👩‍👧 Who's Who?
See a family photo, choose the right name
🕐 My Day
Picture quiz — what comes next in the daily routine?
🏡 Sort It Out!
Simple yes/no — is this food? is this an animal?
Every game has three heart levels: ❤️ easy, ❤️❤️ medium, ❤️❤️❤️ challenge.
Getting Started in 3 Steps
Step 1 — Save the file
Visit the official link and click ⬇️ Download Remember Today:
👉 https://matthewabrahamsoh.github.io/Remember_today
A file called RememberToday.html will save to your Downloads folder.
Step 2 — Open it
Double-click the file. It opens in your browser (Chrome, Safari, Edge — all work).
Bookmark it or add it to your home screen for easy daily access.
Step 3 — Add your photos (takes 5 minutes)
Tap the gold "Open Setup" button on the home screen.
Add three things:
Your loved one's home photo
Family member photos with names
Custom match photos (optional)
Your photos are saved permanently on this device. They will still be there next time you open the file.
How to Add Photos — Step by Step
🏠 Home Photo
This replaces the house emoji 🏠 in Journey Home and Walk in the Park with a real photo of your loved one's actual home.
Open Setup
Under Your Home Photo, tap 📷 Tap to choose photo
Choose a clear photo of the front of the home — taken from the street, the way they would see it when returning
Tap Save & Close
Tip: Use a photo taken in daylight, from the angle they would naturally approach. Seeing their real home makes the wayfinding game therapeutic rather than abstract.
👨‍👩‍👧 Family Member Photos (for Who's Who?)
These appear in the Who's Who? game. The patient sees a face and chooses the correct name.
Open Setup
Under Family Members, tap 📷 Tap to choose photos
Select up to 8 photos — one face per photo works best
After uploading, tap the small text box under each photo and type:
The person's name as your loved one knows them — e.g. "Mum", "Ah Boy", "老师", "Ahmad"
Avoid full formal names if your loved one uses nicknames
Tap Save & Close
Tips:
Use recent, clear, well-lit face photos
Include the people they see most often
Start the Who's Who? game at ❤️ level (2 choices) before increasing difficulty
It is normal for a person with dementia to struggle with faces — this game is practice, not a test
🌟 Custom Match Photos
These replace the Singapore landmark symbols in Memory Match with photos meaningful to your loved one — family members, favourite places, familiar objects.
Open Setup
Under Match Game Photos, tap 📷 Tap to choose photos
Choose 4–8 photos (you need at least 2)
Type a short label under each — e.g. "Ah Gong", "Garden", "Cat"
Tap Save & Close
The game will now use your photos instead of the default symbols.

About the Games
🏠 Journey Home & 🌳 Walk in the Park
The patient taps numbered dots in order, tracing a heart shape.
When they reach the Coffee Shop ☕ or Park 🌳 midpoint, a warm message appears — for the patient, and a gentle tip for the caregiver about using the moment to discuss real landmarks on their actual route home.
Why this helps: Repeated practice of a familiar route builds real wayfinding confidence.
🌟 Memory Match — ❤️❤️ Level (3×3 with Heart Centre)
At the ❤️❤️ level, the centre cell shows your loved one's home photo (if uploaded) surrounded by 8 matching cards. Tapping the centre card shows the message: "You are loved. You are safe."
👨‍👩‍👧 Who's Who?
❤️ Level: 2 name choices (very easy — 50/50)
❤️❤️ Level: 3 choices
❤️❤️❤️ Level: 4 choices
If they get it wrong, the correct name is highlighted in green. No penalty — just gentle correction.
🕐 My Day — Picture Quiz
The patient sees an activity picture and chooses what comes next from 2, 3, or 5 options.
❤️ Level: 1 from 2 (e.g. after Wake Up — Brush Teeth or Bedtime?)
❤️❤️ Level: 1 from 3
❤️❤️❤️ Level: 1 from 5
The sequence follows a real daily routine: Wake Up → Brush Teeth → Breakfast → Medication → Morning Walk → Lunch → Afternoon Nap → Tea Time → Dinner → Bath → TV Time → Bedtime.
🏡 Sort It Out!
Simple yes/no questions with no ambiguity:
❤️ Food only
❤️❤️ Food and Animals
❤️❤️❤️ All four categories (Food, Animals, Sky, Round shapes)
A 💡 Hint button is available — tapping it reveals the answer before guessing. If hint is used, the point doesn't count, but there is no negative feedback.
Tips for Caregivers
Start small. Begin with ❤️ level on whichever game feels most natural for your loved one. Build up over days, not hours.
Play together. Sit beside them. The games are most effective as a shared activity, not something done alone.
Celebrate every answer. Right or wrong, the act of engaging is the therapy.
Use the midpoint messages. When the Journey Home game pauses at the Coffee Shop or Park, use that moment to name real landmarks near their home — "Remember the coffee shop on the corner?" This is where the game connects to real life.
Repeat the same game. Familiarity is comforting. Playing the same game daily builds routine, which is itself therapeutic.
Change the language if it helps. Some patients are more comfortable in their mother tongue. Switch freely.
About the Photos
All photos are stored only on this device, inside the browser's local storage. They are never sent to the internet. If you clear your browser's site data or use a different browser, you will need to re-upload the photos once.
Photos are automatically compressed to a small size on upload, so the file runs smoothly even on older tablets.
